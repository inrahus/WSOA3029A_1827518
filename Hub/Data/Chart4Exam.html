Total Number of Confirmed Cases Each Month in South Africa - April 2020 to November 2020

With this graph, I think I was clearly able to show the extent of my D3 abilities, as well as my overal competency with JS. Although I did struggle, and this can cleary be seen in the size of the graph which I couldn't really figure out how to change wthout my whole graph disappearng. I am howevevr very happy with the result - particuarly in terms of colour, and in the display of interactivity with which I struggled for so long with over the semester.

In this visualisation, I wanted to further show the data in it's individual components. However, an issue I only noticed post completion, is that on return to the main graph, the bars reverse in position. I was unable to address this in time, but I am stil heavily satisfied with the final outcome of my project, especially considering the numerous stuggles I encountered in this section of work thorough the semester. I also ended up removing th elabels I had for the axes, as with the switch in levels, the labels did not correspond and tended to overlap in some cases. Although I feel this would have furthered the graph more, I think that the lack does not heavily take away from the presented information.

With the new undrstanding of tooltips - which I incorporated into my last graph, but did not clearly communicate to the user - I was able to further on this and create a meaningful hover effect that allowed me to showcase the information from the tooltip to the viewer in a more cohesive and interactive style. And I also incorporated the highlighting feature - also used before - to further the interactivity. I also used a cursor change to a pointer so that there was clear indication that the bars could be clicked. In retrospect, I feel that I could have possibly done more to show the clicking aspect of the bar alongside the highlighting.

Another point to note, is that this data set is the same as previous, but with more depth into the individual compnentes that make up the massive number of confirmed cases. I also shifted the range of data fom MArch to November, to April to November. This was because the bar for March was so smal, no interactive compoments could work with it. If I could have figured out the size of my graph in time, I belive I would have left it in, and showcases the full amount of COVID data in South Africa since the beginning of quarantine.

I aso changed the colour scale - although it incorporates colours from the website, I tried to show the increase with a sense of emergency, particularly in the moevement from green to yellow to red. I feel that this does come across, but that if the graph was larger, the severity would be better communicated.

(Also, I couuldn't really figure out the positioning of the "Return"button, but it still works... Sorry about that.)
